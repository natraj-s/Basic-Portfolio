ABOUT ME

Born in India, I split my time growing up between Dubai, India, and finally the US. I was introduced to the PC in my 4th grade, but it wasn't until my uncle bought the first PC in my family sometime in '97 that I had fulltime access to it.

As any other kid introduced to his/her first PC, I was fascinated by it immediately. I was inspired to explore it further than my previous usage sessions in school which only involved some brief coding with BASIC. So obviously, I gravitated towards the numerous games included with my first PC, while simultaneously expressing my creative side through MS Paint. Upon the coercion of my parents, I ended up taking my first HTML course during one of my summers in between school years.

After moving to the US however, this love for video games was further enforced as I got introduced to other game consoles. The freedom available in games like Grand Theft Auto 3 and the underlying creativity in the gameplay of games like Hitman eventually led me to majoring in Computer Science at University of Illinois at Chicago.

While working on projects during my undergrad and on projects as part of work post-graduation, one thing became immensely clear to me: I enjoyed working on the look and feel of things just as much as the technical underlyings of it. During my childhood, I used to spend my waking hours drawing and while, I may no longer be interested in pure art like I was, I never lost that creative itch. I just applied that creative side to other ventures.

Finally, that love for meddling with both the technical and creative side of things has essentially brought me back full circle to where it all began: web development. I fully understand that at some point, I'll have to choose which side of things I really want to focus on and grown in. Until then, I'll continue to try and grow in both as much as possible.
